Talent, The True Value of Github

Matthew Brompton

The True Value of Github.

Microsoft - probably the biggest proprietary software organisation in the world made a bold move this week in purchasing GitHub - proabably the biggest open-source collaboration platform in the world in a move that left many scratching their heads.

Not least of the reasons for this head scratching is the eye watering price-tag. $7.5 billion - more than 30x its annual revenue.

Many leaders and business writers are left astounded, attributing this move to that oh so special word: ‘stategy’. But what is it that Microsoft sees in such a seemingly opposing project? To understand the answer, we have to understand developers.

The developer is a strange creature, existing halfway between the business environment and the highly principled world of open-source. Often they love the idea that they can be paid high sums to work their magic 9-5 and then return home to contribute to a bigger goal of open content by night - sort of a strange twist on Mr. Robot, or Batman.

Microsoft, after years of fighting the open source revolution has recently started to make moves that suggest they understand the value of the open-source world and has become one of the biggest contributors to it and I see their aquisition of Github as a part of this wider strategic movement.

So what is it that is so important about Github?

Many developers have expressed fear that Microsoft will flood Github with Ads, some have questioned the ability of Microsoft to run the project and some suggest Microsoft want to glimpse at the content being developed by some of their biggest rivals - Apple and Linux. Personally I don’t trust Microsoft, however I don’t think this move is as sneaky as some developers beleive. I think, Microsoft is not after the code… It’s after the creators of that code.

The internet is a tool. The biggest resource on the internet, despite popular opinion is not Google, Wikipedia or Lycos (yes, they still exist). The biggest resource online, is humans and this move shows that microsoft understands this.

Exactly how microsoft will utilize this massive strategic advantage is, and will remain a mystery for some time but one thing is for sure, in a world where talent is what is driving company growth Microsoft now have a massive edge.
