Mr. Corbyn: Irish Bombing, Terrorist Sympathising Mugwump.

By Matthew Brompton

Like this post

Jeremy Corbyn... Well well, what can I say? I used to think you were a decent bloke but recently I must say I may have changed my mind. It's like someone has washed away all my positive thoughts about a leader who is kind, compassionate, strong enough to withstand a coup of 172 MP's intent on destroying any hopes the Labour party had of being a left-wing representative party. If only there was a word to describe this cleansing of thought...

Proper Mugwump

Here you are, lording your victory over us doing things like riding the bus. I mean, what sort of future PM really acts like the people he is hoping to lead? Because you are hoping to lead aren't you? I mean, everyone says you don't want to be the leader - that it was just your turn to stand. I certainly don't believe that a reluctant leader, who sees himself as part of the team in anyway can be good at the task. It's not as if there are any books on the issue, or even people at the forefront of the leadership field of expertise suggesting reluctant leaders are a good idea.

All of this is besides the point when I really think about it. How can I, in good conscience, vote for a man who has stood next to another man from the IRA. Never mind the fact that your actions helped work towards the lasting peace that we now all enjoy or that you have repeatedly condemned the actions on BOTH sides of that terrible war.

Yes Jeremy, now we all know you can't hide behind your back-bench anymore. Take your place in the firing line so that we can all take pot-shots at you like those terrorists with which you symapathise take shots at us. Don't get me wrong, it wasn't me, David Cameron called you, Mr. Corbyn the terrorist sympathiser and of course David Cameron would never lie to us. By my reckoning if those pesky terrorists hate pork, anyone who likes bacon so much must be honest.

And then there is my FAVOURITE MP Mr. BJ. Now, I don't know any decisions Boris has made but I do know that he makes me laugh. He called you a mugwump A word I have never heard of but that apparently does have a definition*. If he calls you a Mugwump, a mugwump you must be

So who do you think you are kidding Mr. Corbyn? When you think the Tories are done? Just because you have consistently out-performed Therasa May, properly costed your manifesto and are closing the gap in the polls it doesn't mean you get my vote. Nah, I think I'll give it to that guy who wants to legalise weed... What was his name again?

"a person who remains aloof or independent, especially from party politics. It originates from North American politics and dates back to 1832"
-Google. Congratulations BJ. I like something you said.

Back to reality

If you find yourself using the arguments above please take a second to question your sources. Find something that you truly disagree with, or better still - something you agree with that can be backed up. You have a world of information at your fingertips. You can question us on economics, the welfare state, Tax Avoidance, education or just policies in general. Please do, I could use the mental exercise.

I must confess... The bait was there, I had to take it. I've heard statements like those above repeated so often and heard so little considered and thoughtful response I felt the opportunity could not be missed. I'm sorry, I think.
